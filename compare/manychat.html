Mashal vs ManyChat.

ManyChat built the category for comment-to-DM automation and multi-step chat funnels. Mashal now does comment-to-DM too — but bundled with the daily brief that tells you what's actually working. Here's the honest read on where each one fits, with the pricing and feature lists side by side.

Short answer. If your growth engine is complex, multi-step DM funnels and chatbot flows across Instagram, WhatsApp, and SMS, ManyChat is the specialist — Mashal isn't trying to out-build its flow engine. If what you want is to reply to comments and DMs in one place, turn comments into DMs automatically, and — in the same tool — know which posts and keywords are worth automating, Mashal does that from $15 flat, with no price that climbs as your audience grows. Most creators we talk to want the second thing.

Pricing, side by side.

The models are different in a way that matters. Mashal charges a flat rate per workspace — Conversations (the reply inbox plus comment-to-DM automations) is included from the $15 Creator plan, and the price never moves as your following grows. ManyChat has a genuinely useful free tier, but its paid Pro plan is priced by contact count, so the bill rises as your audience does. And ManyChat is automation-only: there's no analytics brief, competitor tracking, or scheduling in the box.

Where Mashal wins, where ManyChat wins.

Mashal wins on

Automation that comes with the intelligence.

  • It tells you what to automate. Mashal is the only one of the two that also reads your numbers and writes a 6 AM brief — so you automate the posts and keywords you can see are working, instead of guessing. ManyChat automates; it doesn't tell you what's landing.
  • Flat pricing. $15 to $449 per workspace, and it never climbs with your contact count. ManyChat's Pro price scales with audience size, so success makes it more expensive.
  • Comment-to-DM + a real reply inbox from the $15 plan — not a separate subscription stacked on top of your analytics tool.
  • Reads YouTube comments too, alongside Instagram and Facebook. ManyChat doesn't touch YouTube.
  • A whole intelligence layer ManyChat has no equivalent for: competitor tracking, Meta Ad Library reads, ad benchmarking, and a multilingual, culturally-aware brief.
ManyChat wins on

Depth of automation.

  • A full visual flow builder. Multi-step sequences, branching chatbots, drip campaigns, and A/B tests. Mashal's v1 is straightforward keyword-to-DM rules, not a flow engine.
  • More channels. WhatsApp, SMS, Email, and Telegram automation. Mashal's Conversations is Instagram and Facebook DMs and comments (plus YouTube comments) today.
  • E-commerce and integrations. Shopify flows, CRM syncs, Zapier, and a large template library. Mashal doesn't do commerce automation.
  • Years of maturity as the category leader in chat marketing, with a deep template marketplace and community.

Who should pick which.

Pick ManyChat if

Your growth depends on multi-step DM funnels, chatbots, or WhatsApp/SMS automation.

That depth is ManyChat's whole product, and it's very good at it. If you're building branching flows, drip sequences, or e-commerce automations, that's the specialist tool — Mashal's v1 automation isn't trying to match it.

Pick Mashal if

You want comment-to-DM automation and a reply inbox — and, in the same tool, to know what's working.

Reply to comments and DMs, turn comments into DMs on a keyword, and get the 6 AM brief that tells you which posts to ride. Automation plus the intelligence, from $15 flat, at a price that doesn't scale with your following.

Pick both if

You run heavy ManyChat funnels but still want the daily intel.

They don't conflict. Keep ManyChat for the funnels; add Mashal for the morning brief, the competitor reads, and the ad benchmarks ManyChat doesn't do. Mashal reads your accounts from a different angle entirely.

Pick neither if

You don't reply to your audience and don't measure performance.

If nobody's answering comments and nobody's reading the numbers, neither tool earns its fee. Use the platform's native inbox until engagement is actually part of the plan.

A note on where Conversations is today.

In the spirit of the rest of these pages: Mashal's Conversations is new. It does the essentials well — a unified comment and DM inbox, replies, and keyword comment-to-DM automation across Instagram, Facebook, and YouTube — but it isn't a multi-step flow builder, and it doesn't cover WhatsApp or SMS yet. Those are on the roadmap. We'd rather say that plainly than check a box we can't back up. What's genuinely different today is that the automation lives inside the same tool that tells you what to automate — and it's included from the entry plan, not billed by the size of your audience.
